Satoru Iwata

"Above all, video games are meant to be just one thing - Fun for everyone."

Satoru Iwata displaying the gamepad for the Nintendo Wii U.

Dec 1959

Born in Sapporo, Japan.

Apr 1983

Graduates from Tokyo Institute of Technology and joins HAL Laboratories.

Nov 1992

Iwata's projects at HAL saved the company from bankruptcy and brought us Kirby!

Nov 1999

Iwata joins Nintendo as a game developer, leads development of Pokémon and Super Smash Bros.

Sep 2001

Satoru's contributions on Nintendo's next console led to the very successful Gamecube, turning Nintendo a positive profit.

Jul 2002

Promoted to CEO of Nintendo and continues to work on game development.

May/Nov 2004

Iwata led Nintendo in a big year with the release of the DS and the announcement of the Nintendo Wii! Initially the DS' reception was mixed, however Iwata's focus on it's games made the system a staple in mobile gaming.

Apr 2013

Appointed CEO of Nintendo of America.

Apr 2015


Passes away at the age of 55 from complications with bile duct cancer.
